# Lava Boats

**Sail the seas of fire.** Lava Boats adds Crimson and Warped boats - plain and chest variants - that
ride on **lava** exactly like normal boats ride on water. Cross a lava lake, navigate the Nether by
boat, and haul your loot across molten terrain without losing a single item to the flames.

> Now multi-loader: **Fabric - Quilt - Forge - NeoForge** for Minecraft **1.20.1** (1.20-1.20.4 family).

## Features

-  **Ride lava like water** - Crimson and Warped boats float on lava and steer at **full water
  speed**. The hull sits the rider safely above the surface - smooth and stable, no bouncing.
-  **Fireproof boats** - your boat never burns, never breaks, and never takes lava damage. Park it
  in a lava lake and come back to it later.
-  **Rider fire-safety** - anyone seated in a lava boat is **completely fire-immune**: no burning,
  no ignition, not even the flame overlay. Passengers ride through lava unharmed.
-  **Floating drops** - break or drop a lava boat and the item is fire-resistant (Netherite-style)
  and rises quickly to the lava surface instead of sinking into the depths.

## Boats added

| Boat | Recipe |
|------|--------|
| Crimson Boat | 5 x Crimson Planks (boat shape) |
| Warped Boat | 5 x Warped Planks (boat shape) |
| Crimson Boat with Chest | Chest + Crimson Boat |
| Warped Boat with Chest | Chest + Warped Boat |

All four appear in the **Tools & Utilities** creative tab alongside the vanilla boats.

## Installing

Drop the jar for your loader into your `mods` folder, plus its dependencies:

- **Fabric / Quilt** - Lava Boats + [Fabric API](https://modrinth.com/mod/fabric-api) + [Architectury API](https://modrinth.com/mod/architectury-api)
- **Forge / NeoForge** - Lava Boats + [Architectury API](https://modrinth.com/mod/architectury-api)

The lava-riding and visuals are client-side; the fireproofing and rider-safety run server-side.
Install on the **server** for the protection features and on **clients** for the full lava-riding
experience. (On a single-player world you just need it in your one `mods` folder.)
